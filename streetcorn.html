Jake's Cast Iron Street Corn Recipe

There are a lot of street corn recipes out there, but here is my personal adaptation of many plus changes based on experience.

There are about 1 1/2 cups of corn in each can, and there are roughly two servings per can.

Supplies

  • Stove
  • 12" Cast Iron Skillet
  • Spatula

Ingredients

In order of appearance

Any ingredient after the corn can be substituted or omitted, except mayo

  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • (optional) 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 3 cans sweet corn, drained
    • ~15oz each, 4.5 cups drained total
    • Can substitute cut frest corn off cob
  • 1/2 red onion, chopped
    • Can substitute white onion or 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 bell or jalepeno pepper, chopped (any color except yellow)
  • 1/4 cup green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 can tomatoes & green chiles
  • 3 tbsp mayo, more or less to taste
  • 1 tsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup cilantro, chopped
  • 1/2 cup feta, cotija, or queso fresco cheese

Procedure

  1. Coat cast iron with cooking oil and preheat cast iron on high heat. If desired, add minced garlic for aromatics and extra flavor
  2. When oil starts to smoke, add corn and onion
    • A vendor at the Farmer's market once told me, "If you don't have onion and garlic, you might as well not cook"
    • If using onion powder, add later with rest of seasonings
  3. Spread the corn and onion out as thinly as possible on the cast iron
    • DO NOT STIR (yet)
  4. Wait until corn starts popping and getting a good char, THEN stir & spread
  5. Wait 2-3 minutes, stir and spread again
  6. Remove from heat
    • The cast iron will remain hot for up to 30 minutes, this is desired. Use caution
  7. Wait 2-3 minutes, then stir in bell/jalepeno pepper, green onion, tomatoes/green chiles, mayo, lime juice, chili powder, cayenne pepper, and salt
  8. Top with cilantro and cheese of choice and serve directly out of cast iron
